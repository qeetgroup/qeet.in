Qeet ID, the first venture inside Qeet Group, is generally available today. Engineering teams can sign up, install the SDK, and ship production sign-in the same afternoon. The product, documentation, and pricing live at [id.qeet.in](https://id.qeet.in).

We started Qeet ID from a question we kept hearing inside other companies: why does every engineering team still have to choose between developer-friendly auth and enterprise-grade auth? The two have been treated as separate categories of product for fifteen years, and the result is that teams either outgrow their tooling at the worst possible moment or pay enterprise prices long before they need enterprise features. We do not believe that trade is permanent. Qeet ID is what closes it.

The capability surface is what modern teams actually need from identity, in one platform. SSO across more than fifty providers. MFA, including passkeys for phishing-resistant sign-in. RBAC and ABAC for fine-grained access. Stateful sessions, multi-tenant isolation, and audit-ready logging. SDKs for TypeScript, Go, Python, Rust, React, Next.js, and mobile. Drop-in components for the parts most teams should not have to build themselves.

The free tier covers five thousand monthly active users with no credit card. The paid tiers are documented on the site. The compliance posture — SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, GDPR-aligned — is baked into the platform from day one rather than added later.

There is more to do. There will be more integrations, more SDKs, more depth in the platform&rsquo;s edges. The point of generally available is not "the product is finished" — it is "the product is ready for the people who will tell us what to build next." If you are running auth at a team that is growing fast, we&rsquo;d like to hear what you find.
